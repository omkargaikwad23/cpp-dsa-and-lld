I had the Googliness round a couple of days back, which focused mainly on behavioral questions. They asked about my hobbies outside work, how I’ve handled intra-team conflicts, and how I prioritize tasks when two tasks with the same priority come up. They also asked about past failures and what steps I took to prevent them from happening again. Another question was about an instance when I caught a teammate doing something unethical and how I handled it. Overall, the interview was more like a discussion where they were interested in hearing about my past experiences and how I’ve managed similar situations in previous roles. It went really well! The interviewer was friendly and engaging.

# How would you handle a teammate taking credit for the work that you both did together?
"In one of my previous projects, my teammate and I collaborated on optimizing a Spark job to improve processing efficiency. During a team meeting, I noticed that my teammate presented the work as solely their contribution without mentioning my involvement."

"Rather than reacting immediately, I decided to approach them privately. I expressed that I was happy our work was recognized but also mentioned that I contributed significantly. I asked if we could present together in the future to reflect our joint efforts."

"My teammate understood my perspective, and moving forward, we ensured proper credit was given. This experience reinforced the importance of clear communication and advocating for my contributions while maintaining team harmony."
